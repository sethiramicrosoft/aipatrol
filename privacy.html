🛡️ GuardPrompt

Privacy Policy

Effective date: 25 May 2025  ·  Last updated: 25 May 2025

✅ GuardPrompt does not collect, transmit, store, or share any of your data — ever. All processing happens entirely inside your browser, on your device.

1. Who We Are

GuardPrompt is a browser extension that warns users before sensitive data (such as API keys, passwords, and personal information) is submitted to AI tools like ChatGPT, Claude, Gemini, and others. It is developed and maintained as an independent software product.

2. Data We Do NOT Collect

GuardPrompt does not collect any of the following:

  • The text you type or paste into AI tools
  • Any detected sensitive data (API keys, passwords, personal information, etc.)
  • Your browsing history or the websites you visit
  • Your IP address or location
  • Your name, email address, or any identity information
  • Usage statistics, analytics, or telemetry of any kind
  • Cookies or any persistent tracking identifiers

3. How Detection Works (Fully Local)

When you paste text or submit a prompt to an AI tool, GuardPrompt runs pattern matching entirely within your browser using JavaScript. The analysis:

  • Never leaves your device
  • Is never sent to any external server, API, or cloud service
  • Is discarded immediately after the check completes
  • Does not persist anywhere — not in browser storage, not in memory after the check

The extension operates entirely offline. No internet connection is required for detection to function.

4. Permissions Explained

GuardPrompt requests the following browser permissions. Here is exactly why each is needed:

  • Read and change data on specific websites — Required to inject the content script that monitors text input on supported AI sites (e.g., chatgpt.com, claude.ai). The extension only runs on the listed AI platforms — not on all websites.
  • Display notifications — Used to show the warning banner when sensitive data is detected. No notification content is stored or transmitted.
  • Storage — Used only to store your local settings (such as whether the extension is enabled). No sensitive data is ever written to storage.

GuardPrompt does NOT request permissions to read all websites, access your tabs, read your browsing history, or communicate with external servers.

5. Third-Party Services

GuardPrompt does not integrate with, send data to, or communicate with any third-party services, analytics platforms, advertising networks, or cloud providers. There are no third parties involved in the operation of this extension.

6. Children's Privacy

GuardPrompt does not knowingly collect any information from anyone, including children under the age of 13. As no data is collected at all, the extension is safe for users of all ages.

7. Data Retention

Because we collect no data, there is nothing to retain, delete, or export. Your local extension settings (e.g., enabled/disabled state) are stored in your browser's local storage and can be cleared at any time by uninstalling the extension or clearing browser data.

8. Your Rights (GDPR / CCPA)

Since GuardPrompt collects no personal data, there is no personal data to access, correct, delete, or port. If you are located in the European Economic Area (EEA), United Kingdom, California, or any other jurisdiction with data protection laws, GuardPrompt is fully compliant by design — there is simply no data to govern.

9. Open Source Transparency

GuardPrompt's detection rules and logic are available for inspection. The patterns used to detect sensitive data are bundled with the extension in a human-readable rules.json file. You can inspect exactly what the extension scans for by viewing the extension's source files in your browser.

10. Changes to This Policy

If this privacy policy changes, the updated version will be published at this URL with a revised effective date. Any future version that begins collecting data will require explicit user consent and will be clearly communicated before taking effect.

11. Known Limitations

Microsoft Edge restricts third-party browser extensions from running on Microsoft-owned websites, including copilot.microsoft.com. This is a browser-enforced policy outside our control. GuardPrompt cannot monitor activity on Copilot when using Microsoft Edge. For full Copilot coverage, users may install GuardPrompt on a Chromium-based browser such as Google Chrome or Perplexity Comet.
